Module 2: LLM Overview and Foundations

Large Language Models (LLMs) are a type of artificial intelligence designed to understand and generate human-like text. They are built upon vast amounts of text data, allowing them to learn complex patterns, grammar, facts, and nuances of human language.

What is Language Modeling?

At its core, an LLM's primary task during pre-training is **language modeling**. This involves predicting the next word or token in a sequence, given the preceding context. For example, if an LLM sees "The quick brown fox jumps over the...", it attempts to predict "lazy". This seemingly simple task, scaled massively, enables powerful text generation and understanding.

The "largeness" refers to their enormous number of parameters (billions, even trillions) and the vast datasets (trillions of words) they are trained on, often comprising a significant portion of the internet's text.
